The Tweeter is a media art installation
which poses a question:

How can the individual filter
objective, truthful and significant
information from the playground of politics?

 N 

The Tweeter is made up of:

A small-scale ice hockey rink!
Finns love ice & hockey. This is sure to catch everyone's attention, like it's the World Cup in 1995! The rink sets boundaries for the installation, and it's surrounded by faux commercials. Commercials remind of money's affluence.
A plastic copy of Auguste Rodin's The Thinker statue
This resembles the nature of a Finnish man, and it's placed in the middle of the rink for the center piece of the installation. This version of the statue is called The Tweeter. A loud speaker in the statue's mouth mumbles tweets, that members of parliament have recently tweeted on their accounts. Public can also make the statue speak, by sending a tweet with a hashtag '#theTweeter'."
8 Spede-cars!
Besides ice hockey, Finns love Pertti 'Spede' Pasanen's films! Many might recall the 'nearly-impossible-to-drive' two-headed cars from the movie "Uuno Epsanjassa". These miniature cars move around the stage & crash into each other every time a parliament member tweets. These cars represent the 8 parliament parties and their relationships.
And, of course...
...the installation wouldn't work without the help of a computer. The computer sends the most recent tweet to the loudspeaker every minute, and it also controls the Spedecars wirelessly.
